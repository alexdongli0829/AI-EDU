# EduLens Parent Advisor — Domain Knowledge

## NSW OC and Selective School Exams

### OC (Opportunity Class) Test
- For Year 4 students entering Year 5
- Tests: Reading, Mathematical Reasoning, Thinking Skills (General Ability)
- Duration: ~2 hours total
- Placement in accelerated classes at select primary schools

### Selective School Test
- For Year 6 students entering Year 7
- Tests: Reading, Mathematical Reasoning, Thinking Skills, Writing
- Duration: ~3 hours total
- Placement in selective high schools

## Skill Taxonomy

### Reading
- **Inference**: Drawing conclusions from text, reading between the lines
- **Vocabulary**: Word meanings in context, synonyms, antonyms
- **Main Idea**: Identifying central themes and purpose
- **Detail**: Locating specific information in text

### Mathematical Reasoning
- **Number Patterns**: Sequences, rules, algebraic thinking
- **Fractions**: Operations, equivalence, decimals, percentages
- **Word Problems**: Translating text to mathematical operations
- **Geometry**: Shapes, area, perimeter, spatial properties

### Thinking Skills
- **Spatial**: Mental rotation, paper folding, mirror images
- **Analogies**: Identifying relationships between concepts
- **Logic**: Deductive reasoning, syllogisms, puzzles

## How to Interpret Mastery Scores
- **75%+**: Strong — student is performing well, maintain with practice
- **55-74%**: Developing — student understands basics, needs targeted practice
- **Below 55%**: Needs Focus — significant skill gap, prioritize in study plan

## Common Error Types
- **Careless Error**: Student knows the concept but made a silly mistake (misread, miscalculated)
- **Time Pressure**: Student ran out of time or rushed, leading to errors on questions they could solve
- **Concept Gap**: Student doesn't understand the underlying concept
- **Misread Question**: Student didn't read the question carefully (missed keywords like "NOT", "EXCEPT")

## Advisor Communication Guidelines
- Lead with positives: always mention strengths before weaknesses
- Be specific: use actual numbers, not vague qualifiers
- Be actionable: give specific practice recommendations
- Frame weaknesses as growth areas, not deficits
- Never predict exam outcomes
- Never compare to other students
