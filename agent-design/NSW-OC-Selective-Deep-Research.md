## 1. System Overview

NSW operates a two-stage gifted education pathway in the public school system:

```
Year 3-4 → Apply for OC → OC Placement Test (Year 4) → OC Classes (Year 5-6)
                                        ↓
Year 5-6 → Apply for Selective → Selective Placement Test (Year 6) → Selective High School (Year 7-12)
```

Both pathways:
- Are **free** (public education system, no fees)
- Use **computer-based tests** (transitioned from paper in 2025)
- Are administered by the **NSW Department of Education**
- Test dates are typically in **May** each year
- Developed by **Cambridge University Press & Assessment** (contracted test developer)
- Scores are NOT disclosed to parents — only performance band reports

**Governing body:** NSW Department of Education's Selective High School and Opportunity Class Placement Unit

**Key recent change (2025+):** Transition from single-version paper tests to multi-version computer-based tests. Multiple test days (Fri-Sun) with make-up test date. This change enables:
- Multiple test versions (prevents cheating/sharing)
- More flexible scheduling
- Immediate digital marking
- Consistent experience across centres

## 2. OC Placement Test — Detailed Breakdown

### What is an Opportunity Class?
- Located in **88 government primary schools** across NSW
- For **academically gifted Year 5 and 6** students
- **1,840 places** available statewide for Year 5 entry each year
- Two-year placement (Year 5 + Year 6)
- Designed for children in the **top ~9%** of their cohort
- Accelerated curriculum with specialised teaching methods

### Eligibility
- Usually in **Year 4** at time of application (recent change: apply in Year 3)
- Aged between **9 years 5 months and 11 years** at Year 5 start
- Australian citizen, permanent resident, or NZ citizen
- Living in NSW by start of school year

### Test Format (Current — 2026 entry)

| Component | Questions | Time | Weighting | Format |
|-----------|-----------|------|-----------|--------|
| **Reading** | 14 (3 have multiple parts → ~33 responses) | 40 min | 33.3% | Multiple-choice |
| **Mathematical Reasoning** | 35 | 40 min | 33.3% | Multiple-choice |
| **Thinking Skills** | 30 | 30 min | 33.3% | Multiple-choice |

**Total test time:** ~110 minutes (plus breaks between sections)

**⚠️ CRITICAL:** No Writing section in OC test (biggest structural difference vs Selective)

### Key Dates (2026 test for 2027 entry)
- Applications open: Late 2025
- Applications close: February 2026
- Test: **8-9 May 2026** (students attend one day only)
- Make-up test: 22 May 2026
- Results: ~6-8 weeks after test

### Reading Test Deep Dive
- **14 questions**, but 3 have multiple parts (total ~33 response items)
- Passage types: fiction, non-fiction, poetry, magazine articles, reports
- Skills tested: literal comprehension, inference, vocabulary in context, critical evaluation, author's purpose, text structure
- **Challenge:** Passages significantly more complex than standard Year 4 curriculum
- Question style: "Which of these best supports the idea that..." / "What is the author's purpose in using..."

### Mathematical Reasoning Deep Dive
- **35 questions** in 40 minutes (~1.14 min per question — fast pace)
- 5 answer options per question
- Tests **problem-solving ability**, not just arithmetic
- Content areas: number & algebra, measurement & geometry, data & probability, patterns
- **Challenge:** Many questions go beyond Year 4 curriculum. Multi-step word problems requiring application in unfamiliar contexts
- No calculator allowed (paper provided for working out)

### Thinking Skills Deep Dive
- **30 questions** in 30 minutes (1 min per question — very fast)
- 4 answer options per question
- Tests cognitive abilities, NOT school curriculum knowledge
- **No previous knowledge required** (official position)

Six measurable sub-skills (per MockStar's research):
1. **Critical Thinking** — identifying logical fallacies, analysing arguments
2. **Problem Solving** — multi-step reasoning, resource allocation
3. **Spatial Reasoning** — 2D/3D transformations, rotations, reflections (**commonly weakest area**)
4. **Pattern & Transformation** — identifying rules in visual/numerical sequences
5. **Logical Reasoning** — ordering, classification, constraint-based problems
6. **Data Extraction** — quickly reading and interpreting charts and tables

## 3. Selective High School Placement Test — Detailed Breakdown

### What are Selective High Schools?
- **17 fully selective high schools** in NSW
- **26 partially selective high schools** (some selective classes within comprehensive schools)
- **4 agricultural high schools**
- **1 virtual selective provision** (Aurora College)
- Total: ~47 schools offering selective placement
- **4,248 Year 7 places** available statewide each year
- For academically gifted students entering **Year 7**

### Test Format (Current — 2026 test for 2027 entry)

| Component | Questions | Time | Weighting | Format |
|-----------|-----------|------|-----------|--------|
| **Reading** | 17 (3 have multiple parts) | 45 min | 25% | Multiple-choice |
| **Mathematical Reasoning** | 35 | 40 min | 25% | Multiple-choice |
| **Thinking Skills** | 40 | 40 min | 25% | Multiple-choice |
| **Writing** | 1 | 30 min | 25% | Open response (typed on computer) |

**Total test time:** ~155 minutes (plus breaks)

### Key Dates (2026 test for 2027 entry)
- Test: **1-2 May 2026** (students attend one day only)
- Make-up test: 22 May 2026

### Writing Test (Selective only)
- **1 open-response question** — 30 minutes
- Typed on computer (significant change from paper-based)
- **25% weighting** — highly significant
- Types: narrative, persuasive, descriptive, or reflective
- Marked by trained human markers (not AI)
- This is THE differentiator for borderline candidates

### Score System
- Combined score from all 4 sections
- **Cut-off marks** vary by school (determined by lowest score of last admitted student)
- Scores are NOT disclosed — only performance band reports
- Estimated mark ranges (out of ~110):
  - James Ruse (elite): 94-98
  - Tier 1 (top 10): 90+
  - Tier 2: 85-90
  - Tier 3: 75-85
  - Regional: 60-80

## 4. OC vs Selective — Key Differences

| Aspect | OC Test | Selective Test |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| **Target Year** | Year 4 students → Year 5 entry | Year 6 students → Year 7 entry |
| **Sections** | 3 (Reading, Math, Thinking) | 4 (Reading, Math, Thinking, **Writing**) |
| **Writing** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes (25% weight) |
| **Total time** | ~110 min | ~155 min |
| **Places** | 1,840 | 4,248 |
| **Applicants** | ~13,000 | ~15,000 |
| **Acceptance rate** | ~14% | ~28% |
| **School type** | Primary school classes | High school (full school) |
| **Duration** | 2 years (Yr 5-6) | 6 years (Yr 7-12) |
| **Reading questions** | 14 (40 min) | 17 (45 min) |
| **Math questions** | 35 (40 min) | 35 (40 min) |
| **Thinking questions** | 30 (30 min) | 40 (40 min) |
| **Section weighting** | 33.3% each | 25% each |

### Key insight for EduLens:
The OC → Selective pathway is a **natural upgrade path**. A student who starts OC prep in Year 3-4 will naturally transition to Selective prep in Year 5-6. The core skills (Reading, Math, Thinking) carry over — Selective just adds Writing and increases difficulty.

## 5. The OC-to-Selective Pathway

This is the key journey most families follow:

```
Year 3: Start preparation (some families)
  ↓
Year 4: OC Placement Test (May)
  ↓ [~14% get in]
Year 5-6: OC Classes (accelerated learning)
  ↓
Year 6: Selective Placement Test (May)
  ↓ [~28% of applicants get in, but OC students have ~60-70% success rate]
Year 7-12: Selective High School
```

**OC students have a significant advantage** for Selective entry:
- Already screened for high potential
- 2 years of accelerated curriculum
- Exposed to more rigorous academic expectations
- Familiar with timed test formats
- Peer effect (learning alongside gifted students)

**But:** Being in OC doesn't guarantee Selective entry. The student still needs to perform on the day.

### The Non-OC path:
Many students go directly for Selective without OC:
- ~50% of Selective entrants did NOT come from OC classes
- Some students "bloom" later academically
- Some families don't pursue OC due to school travel distances

## 8. Cognitive Skill Taxonomy (Test Design Framework)

### Reading Comprehension Skills
1. **Literal comprehension** — explicitly stated information
2. **Inference** — reading between the lines
3. **Vocabulary in context** — meaning from surrounding text
4. **Author's purpose** — why the text was written this way
5. **Text structure** — how the text is organized
6. **Critical evaluation** — assessing arguments, reliability
7. **Synthesis** — combining information across passages

### Mathematical Reasoning Skills
1. **Number & Algebra** — operations, fractions, decimals, algebraic thinking
2. **Measurement & Geometry** — area, perimeter, volume, angles, shapes
3. **Statistics & Probability** — data interpretation, chance
4. **Patterns & Relationships** — number patterns, function machines
5. **Problem-solving strategies** — working backwards, guess & check, drawing diagrams
6. **Multi-step reasoning** — combining 2+ concepts in one problem
7. **Word problem comprehension** — extracting math from English

### Thinking Skills Sub-categories
1. **Critical Thinking** — logical fallacies, argument analysis
2. **Problem Solving** — multi-step, resource allocation
3. **Spatial Reasoning** — 2D/3D transformations, rotations, reflections, nets
4. **Pattern Recognition** — visual sequences, number patterns, rule identification
5. **Logical Reasoning** — syllogisms, ordering, classification, constraint satisfaction
6. **Data Extraction** — chart/table/graph interpretation under time pressure

### Writing Assessment Criteria (Selective only)
Based on marker rubrics:
1. **Ideas & Content** — depth of thinking, originality, relevance
2. **Structure** — logical organization, paragraphing, cohesion
3. **Language** — vocabulary range, sentence variety, literary techniques
4. **Conventions** — spelling, grammar, punctuation
5. **Audience & Purpose** — awareness of reader, maintaining voice
6. **Engagement** — opening hook, sustained interest, satisfying ending

### Webb's Cognitive Framework (used by MockStar)
MockStar has adopted Webb's Depth of Knowledge framework:
- **Level 1 — Recall**: Basic facts and simple procedures
- **Level 2 — Skill/Concept**: Application of concepts, decisions about approach
- **Level 3 — Strategic Thinking**: Reasoning, planning, justifying
- **Level 4 — Extended Thinking**: Synthesis, analysis across multiple concepts

**Relevance to EduLens:** This framework can enrich our Learning DNA model by tagging questions not just by topic but by cognitive depth required.

## 11. Parent Pain Points (Product Opportunity)

Based on market research and the tutoring industry analysis:

### 1. "My child scored 72%. Now what?"
- Raw scores tell parents nothing actionable
- Two students can score 72% with completely different problems
- Parents want to know: Is it a concept gap? Carelessness? Time pressure? Reading comprehension failing on math word problems?

### 2. "We spend $300/month on tutoring but I don't know if it's working"
- No way to track whether tutoring is actually addressing weaknesses
- Progress reports from tutoring centres are often generic
- Parents can't verify if the money is well spent

### 3. "I don't understand the test format or what's being tested"
- First-time parents (especially migrant families) are overwhelmed
- The official NSW resources are basic and bureaucratic
- Tutoring centres explain in group settings, not personalized

### 4. "My child is doing hundreds of practice papers but not improving"
- Volume-based practice (do more tests) is the default strategy
- Without targeting weak areas, students plateau quickly
- No feedback loop: test → identify gap → target practice → retest

### 5. "I want to help but I don't know how"
- Parents aren't educators; they can't diagnose learning patterns
- Hiring a tutor = outsourcing understanding, not building it
- Parents want to be involved but lack the tools

### 6. "I'm spending $15,000 on tutoring — there must be a better way"
- High spend doesn't correlate with better outcomes
- Digital platforms offer better diagnostics at 1/10th the cost
- But current platforms are test-focused, not insight-focused

### 7. Language barrier
- Many parents are not native English speakers (especially Chinese-speaking families)
- Reading English-only reports is a barrier
- Bilingual/multilingual AI chat could bridge this gap

## 12. Implications for EduLens Design

### Test Engine Needs
1. **OC format:** 3 sections (Reading 14Q/40min, Math 35Q/40min, Thinking 30Q/30min) — all MCQ
2. **Selective format:** 4 sections (Reading 17Q/45min, Math 35Q/40min, Thinking 40Q/40min, Writing 1Q/30min)
3. **Timer with gentle warnings** (not anxiety-inducing)
4. **Question navigator** (compact, not 35 numbered circles)
5. **Scratch paper simulation** (for math working)
6. **Writing editor** for Selective (basic word processor, spell check off)

### Skill Taxonomy for Learning DNA
Must cover both OC and Selective:
- Reading: 7 sub-skills (literal → critical evaluation)
- Math: 7 sub-skills (number → multi-step reasoning)
- Thinking: 6 sub-skills (critical → data extraction)
- Writing: 6 criteria (ideas → engagement) — Selective only
- **Each question tagged with:** topic + sub-skill + cognitive depth (Webb's Level 1-4)

### Error Pattern Classification
5 error types (from our v1 design — validated by market research):
1. `concept_gap` — doesn't understand the underlying concept
2. `careless_error` — knows it but makes execution mistakes
3. `time_pressure` — correct reasoning but ran out of time
4. `misread_question` — comprehension failure on question stem
5. `elimination_failure` — can't narrow down to correct option

### AI Agent Implications
1. **Student Agent** must be Socratic, age-appropriate (Year 4 = 9-10 years old), encouraging
2. **Parent Agent** must handle Chinese/English bilingual conversation naturally
3. **Both agents** need access to Learning DNA for grounded responses
4. **Writing feedback** agent needed for Selective (analyze student writing, provide structured feedback)
5. **AgentCore Memory** for cross-session continuity (remember what was discussed last time)

### Market Entry Strategy
- **Start with OC** (Year 4 → Year 5 entry) — smaller scope, faster to build
- **Expand to Selective** (Year 6 → Year 7 entry) — natural upgrade path
- **Bilingual from Day 1** — English + Chinese (non-negotiable for this market)
- **Price below tutoring** ($30-50/month vs $300/month for centres)
- **Position as complement to tutoring**, not replacement (at first)

### Pricing Insight
MockStar charges $29-99/month. We should consider:
- **Free tier:** 1 diagnostic test + limited AI chat (growth engine)
- **Standard ($39/month):** Unlimited tests + AI reports + basic chat
- **Premium ($69/month):** Full AI chat + Writing feedback + cross-session memory + bilingual
- **Annual discount:** Families typically prep for 6-12 months
